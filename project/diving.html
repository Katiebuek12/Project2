Diving in the Olympics

Diving was introduced into the Olympics in 1904 at the St. Louis Games. The sport was also
known as fancy diving due to the acrobatic stunts performed. Women's diving was added in
1912. For both men and women, they can compete in four different events. In 1984 in Moscow
the ban against China was lifted and they were able to compete as well. Three events
were removed due to the popularity of newer events.

The Events

  • 3 meter springboard- both men and women
  • 10 meter platform- both men and women
  • synchronized 3 meter springboard- both men and women
  • synchronized 10 meter platform- both men and women
